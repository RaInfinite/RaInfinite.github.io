Hidden Goals Are Not Directly Visible

Date: July 6, 2026 | Estimated Reading Time: 6 min | Author: ra_xxx

A search, click, favorite, or cart event is not a person's goal. It is a trace. The real goal usually has to be inferred from a sequence of traces, constraints, and later validation signals.

The safer framing is not "what is the user's true goal?" It is "what are the plausible goal hypotheses, what evidence supports each one, and what future behavior would update the belief?"

Why this matters for agents

A shopping agent that acts on one event too aggressively can become noisy. A useful agent should understand uncertainty, stage, and timing before deciding whether to ask, wait, recommend, or act.
