Questions

A small map of the problems I keep returning to. Some will become essays; some are useful because they stay open.

  1. Open thread

    What makes an AI system useful before the user asks?

    I am interested in the line between helpful anticipation and noisy interruption.

  2. Taste

    How do we evaluate taste?

    A benchmark can count correctness, but it often struggles to see judgment, restraint, and timing.

  3. Evaluation

    When is a benchmark secretly a product philosophy?

    Every scoring rule rewards a way of behaving. I want to make those hidden values easier to inspect.

  4. Human model

    What does it mean to understand a person from traces?

    Clicks, searches, and saves are evidence, not the whole inner life. The gap between them matters.

  5. Agency

    How much autonomy should a shopping assistant have?

    The hard question is not only what it can do, but when it should wait, ask, prepare, or act.
